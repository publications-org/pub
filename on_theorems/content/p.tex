The following is Rolle' Theorem. For all $c \in \mathbb{R}.$

\begin{theorem}
	A real function $f$ is continuous on an interval $[a, b]$, that is properly closed and differentiable on the open interval $(a, b)$, and such that the identity $f(a) = f(b)$ holds.\\Then there exists at least one variable $c$ in the open interval such that:
	\begin{equation}
		f'(c) = 0, \quad c \in \mathbb{R}.
	\end{equation}
\end{theorem}
