\section{Writing a Proof:}

\lettrine[lines=2, lhang=0.1, loversize=0.2]{W}riting a proof involves multiple methods, constructive, chain inference, inference. All of whom shall be used per your theorem' case.\\
One caveat to add, is to know existing proofs and theorems that may help you bridge towards your proof. As how it happened with Fermat' Last Thoerem.\\
The following is a structural example of a proof by construction for a variable $I$ in $\mathbb{C}^{+}.$

\begin{proof}
	Per Theorem N:\\
	(1) We will construct that for $I$ assuming it is an irrational number in $\mathbb{C}$.\\
	(2) Therefore. Let $I^{p} \in \mathbb{C}^{+}.$ Observe that for $Z, Y, I \in \mathbb{C}$:
	\begin{align*}
		I &= Z+Y+i\pi,\\
		I-Y &= Y+i\pi.
	\end{align*}
	(3) Per $i\pi$ being an irrational and transcendental number, per previous steps, summing $i\pi$ into $I$ and for $p \neq 0$ would then proove by construction $I$ to be an irrational number.
\end{proof}
Every step of the proof shall be condtioned per (3), (2). As the importance of closing is really what makes a proof pass or break.
