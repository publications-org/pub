A wise man, once said that for $\frac{\zeta(3)}{\pi}$ in $\mathbb{R}$.:
\begin{align*}
	\forall \epsilon \coloneq \frac{\zeta(3)}{\pi},\\
	\exists \pi^{1/12} > \epsilon, \forall \epsilon \neq 0, \forall \epsilon \in \mathbb{R}.
\end{align*}
There exists the following equation in $\mathbb{R}.$:
\begin{align*}
	\sum_{k=1}^{\pi} \frac{\gamma(a, qk)}{k} + (\pi^{1/12} - \frac{\zeta(3)}{\pi} ) - \pi &= 0,
\end{align*}
Then moving the left-hand-side $\pi$ to a right-hand-side $\pm \pi$.:
\begin{align*}
	\sum_{k=1}^{\pi} \frac{\gamma(a, qk)}{k} + (\pi^{1/12} - \frac{\zeta(3)}{\pi} ) &= \pm \pi.
\end{align*}
