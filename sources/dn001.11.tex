\section{DEFINITION OF $\operatorname{EVol}_{1}(\Delta t, p)$:}

Let the argument $\Delta t$, and the variable $p, \quad p \geq 2$:

\begin{equation}
    \operatorname{EVol}_{1}(\Delta t, p) = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{\operatorname{Dec}_{k}((\Delta t)^{p})}{\operatorname{Diff}_{k}(\Delta t)}, p \in \mathbb{Z}
\end{equation}
Where $\operatorname{Dec}_{k}(x) \in \mathbb{R}$ is the Decremental function of a variable $x$. We also define $\operatorname{Diff}_{k}(x) \in \mathbb{R}$ as the Differential equation of a variable $x$.
