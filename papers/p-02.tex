\section{Conjectures}
The two following conjectures are heavily restrained to certain conditions per written in their respective section.

\subsection{Conjecture One}
For all elements in a set of P, note that:
\begin{equation}
    P_0 < P_{n-1} \xrightarrow{} (x) < (x+1)
\end{equation}
s.t.:
\begin{equation}
    \{ x, \space \cdots, \space (x+p) \} \in P
\end{equation}
for all: $x \in \mathbb{R}$ and $x > 0$, and $x$ belongs to the set of prime numbers.
\subsection{Conjecture Two}
Assuming that $F(t)$ is defined in a interval $I$, note that:
\begin{equation}
    F(t)= \int_{p}^{n} G(x) dt
\end{equation}
s.t. $G(x)$ is always in $F(t)$, granted that $t$ is defined in the integral and $t$ belongs to the set of reals. s.t.:
\begin{equation}
    \forall G(x), \space t \in \mathbb{R}, \exists \space G(x) \in \mathbb{P}
\end{equation}
